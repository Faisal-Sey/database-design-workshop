# Further Reading

Curated list — short enough to actually finish. Start with the top two this week.

---

## Essential (read this week)

**[Use The Index, Luke](https://use-the-index-luke.com/)**
The best free resource on database indexing for developers. Forget the vendor docs — this explains *why* an index works, with diagrams. Read chapters 1–3.

**[PostgreSQL Documentation: Performance Tips](https://www.postgresql.org/docs/current/performance-tips.html)**
Bookmark this. The official Postgres docs are unusually readable. The "Using EXPLAIN" section will change how you debug slow queries.

---

## Schema Design

**[Database Design for Mere Mortals](https://www.informit.com/store/database-design-for-mere-mortals-a-hands-on-guide-to-9780136788041)** — Michael J. Hernandez
The clearest book on relational design that exists. Chapters 1–10 cover everything in this webinar and more. Available in the UG/KNUST library and on Z-Library.

**[SQL Style Guide](https://www.sqlstyle.guide/)**
Free. Opinionated conventions for writing SQL that your teammates can read.

---

## NoSQL & Trade-offs

**[CAP Theorem in plain English](https://mwhittaker.github.io/blog/an_illustrated_proof_of_the_cap_theorem/)**
Five-minute read. Explains why you can't have perfect consistency AND availability in a distributed system — the real reason NoSQL databases exist.

**[MongoDB Schema Design Best Practices](https://www.mongodb.com/developer/products/mongodb/mongodb-schema-design-best-practices/)**
Official guide. Covers embedding vs referencing — the decision that shapes every MongoDB schema.

---

## Performance & Production

**[EXPLAIN Explained](https://www.pgmustard.com/docs/explain)** — pgMustard
Interactive guide to reading PostgreSQL's EXPLAIN ANALYZE output. Paste your slow query's plan and it highlights the problem.

**[The Internals of PostgreSQL](https://www.interdb.jp/pg/)**
Free online book. Go deep here once you're comfortable with the basics — covers MVCC, WAL, vacuum, and how indexes are physically stored.
